# Overview

Cell aging describes the gradual loss of capacity and increase in
internal resistance that occurs as a lithium-ion cell is used. Aging
is driven by calendar time, cycle count, temperature, and operating
conditions.

# Aging Mechanisms

1. **Solid Electrolyte Interphase (SEI) growth** — consumes lithium inventory.
2. **Lithium plating** — metallic lithium deposits on the anode surface.
3. **Particle cracking** — mechanical fracture of active material particles.
4. **Electrolyte decomposition** — chemical breakdown at high temperatures.
